OUR JOURNEY

The Conolly Canal at Kozhikode district in Kerala presented a common yet devastating problem. Amidst all the water security and scarcity issues, that contaminated water body remained as an answer difficult to decipher. This was the sight that sparked the attention of Shilpa, the founder of DIME. The lack of clean drinking water at the hostel that she came across in the year 2015 was that final push that led to her passion in fostering the study, aimed to tackle this behemoth of a problem. The innovative idea for clean water was proposed as an academic project in 2018. Onward in the journey, the immense attempts in collaboration with various sources resulted in the formation of Dime Klear.

OUR MISSION

When the well is dry, we'll know the worth of water" - Benjamin Franklin.

It only takes a good look into the world to see how fast the well is drying up. Over 1.8 billion people do not have reliable access to drinking water, and this number is set to be doubled by 2030, exacerbated by climate change, urbanization, population growth and depletion of water sources with increasing industrial demands.

The current solutions for safe drinking water disenfranchise the largely rural population of India, and that's why we are here. DIME stands to offer its innovation to solve the water crisis in a way that's affordable and accessible to all; because water should never be a luxury. It's a necessity.

OUR PRODUCT

IDEA

It is an ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation)Supported project. Our central idea lies in the concept of water is a necessity, not a luxury. We have developed an affordable, efficient product that uses agriculture material, available in abundance with high-end technology, Nano Technology to convert Seawater to potable drinking water.

INNOVATION

Decontamination of drinking water by using raw materials, which is available in abundance, Is the prime objective of the device. A synergetic system capable of adsorption, as well as the destruction of microbial organisms, is envisaged. ​

•Utilizing the synergistic effector ( the antibacterial property) of the nanoscale particles

•Using adsorption for longer life of the particles embedded in the filter

•Using nanotechnology combined with the efficient mass transfer operation adsorption

UTILITY

Water is the elixir of life, and access to pure potable water is every human being's right. Unfortunately, nowadays, this right has been denied to billions of people worldwide as they do not have access to pure potable water. The most recent WHO report says that "about 1.8 billion people in our world lacks pure potable water", which reveals the gravity of alarming water scarcity and contamination problems in our motherland.

Desalination is the most significant feature it offers, which involves removing salt content from Seawater and making it ready for drinking purposes.

IMPACT
